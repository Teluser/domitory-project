Moving into a dormitory

When filling in your registration form, you need to put a tick next to “I need a dorm and temporary registration for the period of my studies”.

In your personal applicant’s page, you need to attach scanned copies of the following documents:
  • Form 086/y certificate;
  • Medical assessment of a chest X-ray performed in Russia (in case it is not included in your form 086/y certificate);
  • Vaccination certificate.
    • You will also need to provide two copies of the 086/y certificate to the sports medicine specialist in the beginning of September (for your Physical Education classes).

    Within a week after your order of admission to ITMO is issued, instructions on how to sign up to the university’s intranet system ISU will be sent to the email address listed in your personal applicant’s page. The sign-up process is obligatory for all students.

    In August, every student with a guaranteed place in one of the university’s dormitories can book the date and time when they would like to move in. Students will be notified by email when this feature becomes available, and the date and time of move-in during the first wave will need to be booked within 48 hours after the student is assigned to a dorm. Attention: you cannot choose the dorm you will be assigned to. Before the residential lease agreement takes force (on September 1), the applicant has to pay 200 rubles/day for their stay at the dormitory.

    In late August, the moving-in process will start in accordance with the Order of precedence of providing accommodation. To move into an ITMO University dormitory, you need: Your passport and a copy of it; Notarized parental consent in accordance with form 1 (in case the applicant is below 18); Two 3x4 cm photos. To move into the dorms of the Interuniversity campus and the St. Petersburg State Forestry University, you need: Your passport and a copy of it; Parental consent on signing the residential lease agreement and residence in the Interuniversity campus in accordance with form 2 (this one doesn’t have to be notarized; needed for students below 18); Certificate of student status, acquired at the Student Services Office; Two 3x4 cm photos; Copy of a form 086/y certificate; Copy of a medical assessment of a chest X-ray performed in Russia (in case it is not included in your form 086/y certificate); Copy of a vaccination certificate; Certificate from the university’s medical office. You can only move in after you have attached scanned copies of your medical documents on your personal applicant’s page. If the documents haven’t been attached, you need to provide their copies (form 086/y, medical assessment of a chest X-ray performed in Russia (in case it is not included in the 086/y certificate), vaccination certificate, the medical insurance policy) to the university’s medical office. To book the date and time of their move-in, international students need to inform the International Students Learning and Support Center about the date and time of their arrival not later than three days in advance at: int.students@itmo.ru.

    If a student hasn’t been assigned a place in one of the university’s dorms in the first wave, they will have to find a temporary residence in St. Petersburg on their own (or with the assistance of an estate agency) and keep tracking their place in the queue on their personal ISU page (My Page - Education - Dormitory) until their status changes to “place assigned”.
